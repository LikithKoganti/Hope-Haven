Find Support with Hope Haven

Hope Haven helps people in Connecticut quickly find the support they need. Use this website to explore nearby shelters, free food programs, and volunteer opportunities.

  • Look up safe shelters when you or someone you know needs a place to stay.
  • Discover food pantries, community meals, and grocery support programs.
  • Learn how to volunteer and give back to your neighbors in need.

Our goal is to connect you with real people and local organizations that care.

Hope Haven partners with local volunteers and community groups who sort food, prepare bags, and support families every day. When you visit our site, you can find these programs or sign up to help them.

How to Use This Page

  1. Decide what you need right now – a shelter, food support, or a way to help.
  2. Visit the ShelterLink page to search for open beds and warming centers.
  3. Use the Food section to find food pantries and community meals.
  4. Go to Volunteer & Donate if you want to sign up as a volunteer or donate supplies.

You can move between these sections using the green menu at the top of the page. The page you are on will always be highlighted so you know where you are.

What You Can Find Through Hope Haven

Shelter Resources

Night shelters, family shelters, emergency warming centers, and support for people leaving unsafe situations. Many locations list the number of beds available and who they serve.

Food Support

Food pantries, mobile food trucks, community dinners, and school food programs. We focus on options that are free or low cost and open to everyone in need.

Volunteer & Donate

Places where you can help pack food, serve meals, organize clothing drives, or support shelters. Giving even a few hours a month can make a big difference.

If You Need Urgent Help

If you are in immediate danger, always call 911 first. For non-emergency help, you can also:

  • Call 2-1-1 Connecticut to talk with someone and get connected to local services.
  • Visit your nearest community center, library, or town hall to ask about local support.
  • Use this site on a phone, tablet, or computer to find programs near your city or ZIP code.
