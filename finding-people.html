How Volunteer Match Works

Not everyone in need has internet access or knows about the Hope Haven website. Volunteer Match helps volunteers find and assist people by letting volunteers report needs they observe in the community and coordinate help with other volunteers.

1

Observe or Learn About a Need

A volunteer may notice someone who needs help (food, shelter, transportation, medical assistance, etc.) or hear about a need through shelters, community centers, or word-of-mouth.

2

Report the Need

Use the “Report a Need” form to describe the type of help needed, urgency level, and a general area (cross-streets/neighborhood). Avoid exact addresses and personal details.

3

Volunteers Find Requests

Other volunteers can browse and filter requests by type, status, and area to find nearby ways they can help.

4

Claim and Resolve

Claim a request to avoid duplicate help, coordinate support, and mark it as resolved once assistance has been provided.

Important Notes

  • Do not share exact addresses or sensitive personal information.
  • If someone is in immediate danger, contact emergency services.
  • This is a prototype — requests are saved in this browser only.
  • In a real deployment, requests would be moderated and stored securely.
