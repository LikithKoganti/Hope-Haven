Planning Analysis Sheet

Goal, structure, content sources, and site flow for the Hope Haven website.

Website Goal

The goal of the Hope Haven website is to provide a single, easy-to-use place where people in need can find food, shelter, and community support resources, and where volunteers and donors can discover meaningful ways to help.

What results do we want to see?

  • People in crisis can quickly locate nearby food pantries and shelters.
  • More volunteers sign up to support local programs through our forms.
  • Donors understand where and how to donate responsibly.
  • Visitors learn about homelessness, food insecurity, and ways to take action.

Working Titles of Web Pages

  • Home – “Hope Haven: A Community Resource Hub”
  • Find Resources – “Find Food & Support Near You”
  • Food – “CT Food Finder (Pantries & Free Programs)”
  • Find Shelter – “Shelter Search & Emergency Support”
  • ShelterLink – “ShelterLink Innovation & Market Analysis”
  • Volunteer & Donate – “Give Time, Share Hope”
  • Volunteer Application – “Volunteer Sign-Up Form”
  • Donate – “Donation Form & Support Options”
  • About Us – “Our Mission and Story”
  • Community Stories – “Stories of Hope and Resilience”
  • Contact – “Connect With Hope Haven”
  • Planning Analysis – “Planning Analysis Sheet & Site Flowchart”
  • Initial Reflection – “Initial Community-Engaged Learning Reflection”
  • Final Reflection – “Final Community-Engaged Learning Reflection”

Information Needed

What information do we need?

The website needs accurate resource information (food and shelter programs), clear guidance for users, and supportive content for volunteers/donors. Information must come from credible directories and official community sources.

Page Information Already Have Information Needed & Sources
Home Draft mission and website description. Final mission statement + brief facts on homelessness/food insecurity. Sources: government/nonprofit reports and public statistics.
Find Resources Resource categories and navigation layout. External resource links + local directories. Sources: FindHelp.org, CT 2-1-1, nonprofit/community pages.
Food Food finder page layout and map concept. Pantry/program listings, hours, and eligibility details. Sources: food bank directories, nonprofit sites, community listings.
Find Shelter Shelter search UI concept. Shelter contacts, services, and availability details. Sources: official shelter/agency websites and trusted directories.
ShelterLink (Innovation) Initial concept/features for an innovative tool. Competitor research, adoption strategies, risk table, Lean Canvas, survey + collected data. Sources: online research + student survey.
Volunteer & Donate Volunteer/donation page structure and forms. Verified partner needs (roles, schedules, donation rules). Sources: partner org pages and volunteer program guidelines.
Community Stories Story card layout idea. Approved stories, anonymized examples, photos (royalty-free or original). Sources: public stories or instructor-approved content.
Contact Contact form design. Accurate email/phone/office details (if provided) + external support links.
